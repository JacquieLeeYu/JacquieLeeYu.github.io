Time Machine

Jan 2022 - May 2022

My team and I made this prototype for a scheduling app through my HCI class in Purdue in order to learn the process of designing an app and how certain features will be received by users. Verse

Jan 2020 - May 2020

I also made this website called Verse with a team during my Spring 2020's Software Engineering class. In this class, we were supposed to spend the semester building one project while setting milestones and following a sprint format. At the end of the semester, we created Verse, a website that takes in data dumps from popular websites such as Facebook, Google, and Spotify, parses them, and then presents them as a digestible report for the user to read through. This was the first time we tried to use React JS and Django, and there was no shortage of issues trying to set up the darn things, but we persevered and we ended up with this as our finished project. We wanted something that could show people popular platforms had a lot more data on us than people would think, and I think a more polished version of this could work in the real world. All in all, this project was a fun ride and I'm proud of what we accomplished.

Plague Predictor

Jan 2020

I made this app with my team during BoilerMake's 7th annual hackathon at Purdue. This app was created to analyze symptoms that the user has put into it, and tell them whether or not they had a certain plague that was starting to make headline news. We utilized a Naive Bayes classifier and 2000 sets of training data to train the app, and though the outcome of the process could have been better, I'd say it was a good first effort in combining Python code with Javascript.
