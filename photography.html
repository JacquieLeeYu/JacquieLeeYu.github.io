The City of London

London, UK

I visited London for the first time in October 2022 as a part of a week-long vacation where I would be in London for the first half of the week and Paris for the latter half. At the recommendation of my sister, I went to many museums and other famous locations throughout the city, including The Tower of London, the London Natural History Museum, and Hyde Park. As is clear with the amount of pictures from Hyde Park, that place was the most enjoyable part of my visit. I think this is proof I'm more of a nature person than a city person.

The City of Paris

Paris, France

I visited Paris for the first time right after London. My sister and I took the Eurostar train to Paris, and it was the worst because my ears kept on constantly popping the entire ride there. I do like the architecture of Paris quite a lot, and it seems many of the buildings have a beige color, giving the city a warm vibe. Like with London, I also visited the prominent sites in the city, like the Louvre, which was a LOT bigger than I thought it was. Despite the beautiful sights and delicious food, I do prefer London to Paris, simply because 90% less people smoke in London.

The Woodland Zoo

Seattle, WA

I went to the Woodland Zoo in August 2022 and saw all the animals there. I think my favorite ones are the monkeys and birds. It was cool to see them look at me as if I was the sight to behold and not them. I guess they don't see a big camera making faint clicking sounds all too often.

Japan

Tokyo and Kyoto, Japan

I visited Japan in July of 2019, and spent most of my time in and around Tokyo, but I also took a day trip to Kyoto as well. The country is quite clean, though transportation is limited to the subway, since taxi fares are too expensive to take. Most of the time, I just walked everywhere, which led to my feet being sore for nearly all of my time there. All in all, though, Japan is an immensely beautiful place to visit, and I'd like to go again soon.

Purdue University

West Lafayette, IN

I attended Purdue University from 2018-2022 (though half the time I had to stay home due to "The Bad Thing" that happened). I made a lot of friends, learned a lot of things, and did a lot of new things. College is truly the time to spread your wings and try new things. It's full of ups and downs, but at least I came out of it with a degree!

Chicago

Chicago, IL

I actually visited Chicago twice during my time in Purdue: once in August 2018 and once in January 2022, which are arguably the worst times for me to EVER visit this city. January makes sense, since they don't call Chicago "The Windy City" for no reason. Yet, for some reason, for the August I was there, it was sweltering hot and without a single wisp of wind. Very nice architecture, though.
