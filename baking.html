Cakes and The Artistry Required

Classic Cakes

January 7th, 2020

I think I first started baking cakes around 2020, when my friend invited me over to bake a cake together. It was a four-layered chocolate cake with a chocolate ganache coating on the outside. We ended up dusting matcha powder on top for some reason I can't recall, other than the fact that maybe we just had it on hand at the time and thought it was something fancy we could put on. Then, we thought to decorate the outside with canned whipped cream since we saw most cakes looked like that, but sadly, they all deflated quickly. The cake did taste good though, so I'll consider it a success.

May 10th, 2020

The next time I made a cake, it was a three-layered cake with a larger amount of matcha added in on the lower layers. I made this for Mother's Day, which was when we had a big family reunion. Although I did mean to make a giant cake, I didn't mean to make one that big. I used an icing made from cream cheese this time, since I didn't want it to melt halfway through the cake construction like the last cake (although whipped cream not from a can might have worked anyway). The cake itself turned out kind of dense and a little dry, which may be the result of adding so much matcha powder in the first place, though this did not deter me from trying more matcha flavored desserts in the future.

August 10th, 2020

This time, I tried to make a single layer matcha cake, with a bit of cocoa powder sprinkled on top of the cake to see if it worked well together (it did not). I think I once again failed to incorporate matcha powder into the recipe, as the bottom of the cake was dense and still too dry. I'm not sure what possessed me to make such a weird cake, but I think I was just feeling like making a cake that day. I did use whipped cream as the icing this time, since the other types of icing (butter and cream cheese) were way too flavorful for my tastes. In fact, most of my cake icings from now on are made from heavy cream for its mildly sweet flavor.

August 17th, 2020

The cake I made this time was created to see just how little I could put into a cake and have it still taste good. The base cake layers were made the same way, but I did as little frosting as possible (though I did add cocoa powder because I felt like it). I put a very thin layer of icing between the two layers, and did the same for the outsides as well. My family dislikes cakes with too much sweetness (like a full chocolate cake), so I wanted to check if just cocoa powder dusted on top would work for a "chocolate" cake. The short answer? yes but also no. The long answer? Although the cocoa powder worked for tasting like chocolate, it has to sit on your tongue before you can taste it. The cocoa powder is also only on the top of the cake, so if you only bite the bottom layer, you just get your standard, vanilla cake. I think the only saving point for this cake was the fact that the cake layers were really well done and nicely moist, to the point that it made up for the measly droplet of icing between the cake layers. Altogether, I think this cake is a 5/10, though it was a nice learning experience.

August 20th, 2020

This time, I decided I wanted to make tiramisu, one of my top two most favorite cake. It was my first time making tiramisu, so when I read that making tiramisu required raw egg yolk, a kind of cheese, and alcohol, I was a little more than shocked. No one had ever told me tiramisu had alcohol in it, let alone raw egg yolk and cheese. Which, now that I think about it, I feel like bakeries should put up a sign or something so little kids don't eat it like I did (or maybe store) tiramisu don't have alcohol in it? I dont' know). But nevertheless, I baked the cake layers, which were the standard no flavor cakes I make, dipped them in an espresso and cognac mix, and stacked them on top of each other with mascarpone, egg yolk, and heavy cream in between. Put it all together and you create tiramisu! I covered the top with espresso powder, and then left it in the fridge to cool overnight. When I took it back out to try the next day, I realized two things: one, stacking the tiramisu inside a solid cake pan was a bad idea, and two, I think I just made the best cake in my life. I carefully took out a slice of tiramisu and took a bite. It was the most amazing bite of tiramisu I've ever had, and I don't know if it really was or if I'm biased towards my own cake creation, but I polished that plate within two minutes. The only thing that I would improve now that I've made more since (you'll see below), is that I should really dip the cake in more espresso and cognac.

May 31st, 2021

Next, I made a strawberry cake and tiramisu cake to celebrate my grandma's birthday. The strawberry cake was just a simple vanilla base with strawberries lining the space between the two cake layers and along the outside icing. The tiramisu cake is pretty much the same, though I did increase the amount of cognac and espresso added to the cake and I built the cake without the cake pan. I thought since the recipe asked for me to build it in a cake pan, the tiramisu would collapse if I tried to stack it, but it seemed completely fine, so I made all my other tiramisu without it afterwards. As for the strawberry cake, the reason I didn't add too many other things to it was to make it easier for my grandma to eat, since she doesn't typically like to eat cakes that are overly sweet or had too many small things in it. So, when I saw her eating my cake, I was very happy! Watching people eating lots of my food is the best feeling, because it's the perfect way to compliment a chef without telling them explicitly.

June 28th, 2021

This time, I made a smaller strawberry cake, since I was feeling like making slightly sweet but also tart as a little cool snack for the summer heat. Since I was living in my apartment just ourside Purdue, I just made a 6 inch cake so that I could finish it by myself without wasting any of it. I was experimenting with how I should place the strawberries and if I should cut them or not. Here, I cut them in half and then in half again when slicing the cake open. That way, you could see a nice cut of the strawberry instead of just white cream. To know where to cut, I lined the strawberries on top of the cake so that the middle of the space between them was where you were meant to cut. It also helps that even if I forgot that it was a marker, I would be more likely to cut between the strawberries instead of cutting the fruit itself in half. So I think this kind of strawberry lining pattern is quite nice (though it would look better if the cake was bigger).

March 13th, 2022

This cake was actually a birthday present for one of my friends at Purdue. I made her her favorite chocolate mousse cake with a chocolate ganache coating and dark and white chocolate shavings on top. This was also my first time making it, but I think it really turned out a lot better than I thought it would! I used the recipe that is on the back of every Hershey's cocoa powder to make the chocolate cake layer, and then I made the chocolate mousse. I had never used so much chocolate in my life, but this chocolate mousse required a lot more chocolate than I ever thought it could contain, but man, is it absolutely delicious! Even during the making of the chocolate mousse, I had such a hard time not just eating the mousse straight out of the bowl. Thankfully, I reigned myself back in and finished stacking the cake. When it came time to pour the ganache on top, I was so scared I was going to ruin all the previous hard work, I had to triple check that everything was correct before pouring the melted chocolate mix on top. When I revealed the cake to my friend, she was so surprised that it was such an over the top cake (I think she was expecting a small chocolate cake or something) she thought that I bought it at a bakery or something, which was very lovely way to compliment my cake and it makes me warm inside to hear.

April 18th, 2022

Here, I made another tiramisu cake, and although this is the first one depicted here where I made a tiramisu cake in my apartment in Purdue (you can tell because of the small size of the cake) I have actually consistently made tiramisu cake for my friends, since they fell in love with it the first time I served them some. I also like making tiramisu cake, since it's so easy to make it. You just bake the cake layers, assemble the mascarpone icing, and then plop it all on top and wait four or five hours. The simplicity of it brought me to make it over and over whenever I wanted a slight sweet and slightly bitter treat after classes were over. It is also my favorite cake, so I may have gained weight eating so much tiramisu cake, to my disappointment.

Not Quite Classic Cakes

April 16th, 2020

The earliest non-cake cake I made was a swiss roll (or more like an attempt at one) in April of 2020. I was feeling like eating a swiss roll, so I rolled up my sleeves to try my hand at making one. And well, I can say for a fact that swiss rolls are quite hard to nail down. The first time I made this, as seen in the picture to the right, the cake layer is so thin, you might as well be eating a wafer, and the color of it makes you want to throw up on principle. I'm not quite sure if the flavor of the cake was supposed to be chocolate or if I burnt it, since the icing flavor I distinctly remember to be matcha flavor since I apparently had a habit of trying to make it work. I feel like I would remember if I burnt the thing, but I'm also not sure why I would try to make chocolate and matcha work again. The cake also kept breaking apart as I was working with it, so altogether, this was a failure of a swiss roll. But it will not be the last time I made a swiss roll.
